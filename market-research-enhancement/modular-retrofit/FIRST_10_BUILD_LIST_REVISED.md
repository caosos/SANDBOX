# First 10 Build List — Revised

## Verdict

The concept is usable, but the execution plan needs tightening.

Do not directly clone a branded commercial sleeve for sale.
Do not assume one 2 lb silicone kit makes 10 full-size parts.
Do not embed electronics until the passive plug/collar survives mechanical testing.

The first 10 should prove the retrofit carrier/collar, not the final commercial insert design.

## Correct First-10 Product

A prototype retrofit system made of:

1. purchased removable insert used as reference or temporary component
2. molded outer plug/carrier
3. reinforced collar/socket
4. optional external low-voltage motor test module later

The product being proven is the retrofit system.

## Source Inserts

Buy 10 to 15 removable replacement inserts designed for dolls, not only generic standalone sleeves.

Reason:

- doll replacement inserts are closer to the target form factor
- generic standalone sleeves may be too bulky or shaped for another housing
- factory doll removable inserts already prove the size category

Reference price:

- Coeros WM TPE/Silicone removable inserts: $29.50 to $34.50
- related replacement inserts on same page: about $22.80 to $49.80

Important market clue:
Coeros says these inserts are compatible with dolls that already have removable options, not fixed configurations.

## Commercial Sleeve as Master

Using an existing branded sleeve as a private bench reference is useful.

Using it as a direct production master for sold goods is risky.

Risk areas:

- copyright/design-copy/trade-dress claims
- patent claims
- marketplace takedowns
- supplier disputes
- loss of defensibility

Use purchased sleeves for:

- fit study
- softness comparison
- general dimensional reference
- prototype inspiration

Create original geometry before selling.

## Mold Materials

### Mold Star 15 Slow

Good for molds. Not the final product material.

Known specs:

- platinum silicone
- 1A:1B by volume
- 50 minute pot life
- 4 hour cure time
- 15A hardness
- 23.5 cubic inches per pound

Important warnings:

- latex gloves inhibit cure
- sulfur-containing clay inhibits cure
- small compatibility test recommended
- release agent can help, especially silicone-on-silicone

### Dragon Skin 10 Medium

Candidate for prototype parts.

Known specs:

- 1A:1B by weight or volume
- 20 minute pot life
- 5 hour cure time
- 10A hardness
- 25.8 cubic inches per pound
- 1000% elongation
- 102 pli tear strength
- skin safe/certified by independent lab per Smooth-On page
- still labeled for industrial use only

Important warning:
Do not make customer-facing body-contact claims without documentation, testing, and legal review.

## Material Quantity Reality Check

Dragon Skin 10 Medium has specific volume of about 25.8 cubic inches per pound.

A 2 lb kit gives about 51.6 cubic inches total.

If one prototype part uses:

- 5 cubic inches: 2 lb kit can make about 10 parts
- 8 cubic inches: 2 lb kit can make about 6 parts
- 10 cubic inches: 2 lb kit can make about 5 parts
- 15 cubic inches: 2 lb kit can make about 3 parts

Therefore, one 2 lb kit might make 10 small parts, but not necessarily 10 full plug/carrier units.

Recommendation for first 10:

- minimum: 4 lb casting silicone
- safer: 6 to 8 lb casting silicone
- plus mold silicone separately

## Motor Version

Do not make the first 10 all powered.

Better split:

- 7 passive mechanical units
- 3 powered test units

Motor approach:

- encapsulated 3V motor for proof test
- external battery/control pack
- wires exit through rear of plug
- strain relief required
- no buried battery
- no Bluetooth for first round
- no heat yet

Do not sell powered units until tested for water exposure, wire strain, current draw, heat, connector failure, and cleaning workflow.

## Weekend Build Claim

Ten crude prototype castings in a weekend may be possible after materials arrive if molds are already built and cured.

But ten useful tested prototypes in one weekend is optimistic.

Realistic first-10 timeline:

- Days 1-3: buy/receive materials and references
- Days 4-7: build masters/test blocks/mold boxes
- Days 8-10: make molds and cure
- Days 11-14: cast first parts
- Days 15-21: revise, retest, and build the first 10 usable prototypes

## Final Corrected Build Rule

Buy the reference insert.
Build your own plug.
Build your own collar.
Make only 3 powered test units first.
Do not copy a branded sleeve for sale.
Do not move to customers until the collar survives stress and cleaning tests.

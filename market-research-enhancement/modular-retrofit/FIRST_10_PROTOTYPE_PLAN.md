# First 10 Prototype Plan

## Objective

Build the first 10 proof-of-concept retrofit plug/collar units using purchased off-the-shelf removable inserts as fit/reference components.

The goal is not to copy another company's insert. The goal is to prove the outer retrofit adapter system: collar, plug, flange, retention, cleaning path, and install process.

## Core Strategy

For the first 10, buy existing removable inserts and build around them.

Do not begin by trying to sculpt or manufacture the most lifelike internal insert. Let existing inserts solve that temporarily while the prototype proves the retrofit system.

Product under test:

- outer retrofit plug/carrier
- reinforced collar/socket
- removable purchased insert compatibility
- cleaning/drying process
- installation workflow

## Why This Is Faster

Existing replacement inserts are already cheap enough for prototype work. The hard problem is not making a soft insert. The hard problem is making a fixed-opening doll accept a removable modular system without tearing, leaking, trapping moisture, or failing under stress.

## Current Reference Evidence

Coeros lists WM TPE/Silicone removable inserts at about $29.50 to $34.50 and explicitly says they are compatible with dolls that already have removable vaginal options, not fixed vaginal configurations.

Coeros also lists related replacement insert products at roughly $22.80 to $49.80.

YouQDOLL explains that fixed versions are permanently molded into the doll body, while insert versions have a slightly wider internal opening to accommodate a removable sleeve and are easier to clean.

This supports the retrofit gap: replacement inserts exist, but fixed-opening dolls are not automatically compatible.

## Prototype Materials

### Soft Casting Material

Buy enough silicone/soft elastomer for:

- 10 sellable-looking prototype plug/collar units
- 5 to 10 failed parts
- test coupons
- adhesion tests
- tear tests

Budget:

- minimum: $250 to $500
- safer: $500 to $900

Notes:

- Ecoflex 00-30 is soft and has a 4-hour cure time.
- Dragon Skin 10 Medium is stronger and has higher tear strength but is firmer.
- Run small cure-inhibition tests before pouring full units.

## Mold Plan

### Do Not Mold Directly From a Purchased Insert for Sale

Using a purchased commercial insert as a direct copy can create intellectual property and design-copy risk.

Allowed use for early private prototyping:

- measure fit
- understand approximate dimensions
- test carrier geometry
- verify retention concepts
- compare material behavior

Commercial product should use original geometry.

## First Mold Set

Make:

- 2 outer plug molds
- 2 collar molds
- 1 test-block mold
- 1 flange/retention-ring mold

You do not need 12 to 15 molds for the first 10. You need enough to learn fast.

Budget:

- dirt-cheap hand-built molds: $100 to $300
- better printed/finished molds: $300 to $800

## Prototype Build Path

1. Buy 10 to 15 existing removable inserts.
2. Measure length, flange size, outer diameter, taper, and stiffness.
3. Design an outer plug/carrier that accepts one common insert size.
4. Make a test block that simulates the doll opening.
5. Mold the reinforced collar/socket.
6. Mold the outer plug/carrier.
7. Fit purchased insert into carrier.
8. Test retention and removal.
9. Test cleaning/drying.
10. Pull-test until something fails.
11. Record the failure.
12. Revise the collar.

## First 10 Deliverable

At the end of this phase, the deliverable should be:

- 10 passive retrofit plug/collar prototypes
- 1 compatibility test block
- written installation notes
- failure notes
- photos/videos of non-explicit engineering tests
- list of design changes for prototype round 2

## Pass/Fail Criteria

A prototype fails if:

- the collar tears
- the carrier collapses
- the insert cannot be removed cleanly
- cleaning/drying is impractical
- the plug slips out too easily
- moisture gets trapped behind the collar
- adhesive bond fails
- the edge creates a tear path

A prototype passes if:

- the insert seats securely
- the insert removes without damaging the collar
- cleaning/drying is practical
- the collar survives repeated cycles
- failure points are visible and controllable

## Best First Offer

Do not sell the first 10 as final customer products.

Use them for:

- anonymous owner interviews
- private prototype feedback
- compatibility research
- waitlist demo
- investor/product photos
- controlled beta, if legally and safely structured

## Core Takeaway

For the first 10, buy the lifelike insert component instead of manufacturing it.

Build the retrofit plug and collar around it.

The insert proves the feel.

The plug proves the product.

The collar proves the business.

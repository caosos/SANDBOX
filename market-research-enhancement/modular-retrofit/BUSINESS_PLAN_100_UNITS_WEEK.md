# Bootstrap Business Plan: 100 Units Per Week

## Objective

Build a low-capital, garage-style production path capable of producing 100 units per week for a modular retrofit product line.

This plan assumes the first sellable product is a passive, non-powered component or kit. Powered 5V modules come later after the mechanical product survives testing.

## Product Sequence

Do not start with the hardest product.

Recommended launch order:

1. Passive removable insert / replacement insert
2. Cleaning and drying kit
3. Retrofit assessment service
4. Passive retrofit collar/socket kit
5. Controlled install service
6. 5V powered insert module

The fastest path to 100 units/week is not the full retrofit service. It is a standardized passive component or kit that can be molded, trimmed, inspected, bagged, boxed, and shipped.

## Production Formula

Good units per week = molds × cavities per mold × casting cycles per day × production days × yield

Target: 100 good units/week

Assumptions:

- 5 production days/week
- 20 good units/day
- 2 casting cycles/day if cure time allows
- 80% to 90% usable yield early on

Example:

- 12 one-cavity molds
- 2 cycles/day
- 5 days/week
- 85% yield

12 × 1 × 2 × 5 × 0.85 = 102 good units/week

This is the garage-level math.

## Minimum Mold Count

For one-cavity molds:

- bare minimum: 10 molds
- realistic early target: 12 to 15 molds
- better target after demand is proven: 20 molds

For two-cavity molds:

- bare minimum: 5 to 6 molds
- realistic early target: 8 molds

Single-cavity molds are easier to make and repair. Multi-cavity molds are faster but more expensive and less forgiving.

## Minimum Startup Cash

### Dirt-Floor / Garage Minimum

Estimated cash needed: $5,000 to $8,000

This assumes:

- workspace already exists
- owner labor is free
- tools are cheap or already owned
- molds are made in-house
- first product is passive
- no paid employees yet
- no large ad budget
- no expensive tooling
- no full e-commerce buildout

This can start production, but it is tight. It does not leave much room for mistakes.

### Realistic Bootstrap Minimum

Estimated cash needed: $12,000 to $18,000

This is a better number if the goal is to actually hit 100 units/week with fewer delays.

This includes:

- material buffer
- mold failures
- extra supplies
- packaging
- test equipment
- basic website/landing page
- anonymous customer discovery
- shipping supplies
- replacement tools
- scrap/rejects

### Powered 5V Version Minimum

Estimated cash needed: $20,000 to $35,000+

This includes:

- low-voltage electronics
- waterproof connectors
- controller housings
- wire strain relief
- temperature/current testing
- extra failure testing
- higher reject rate
- more liability exposure

Do not start here unless the passive version already sells or preorders are strong.

## Website and Payment Setup

This product category may face payment processor and platform restrictions.

Start with:

- private landing page
- email/waitlist capture
- manual invoicing if needed
- careful terms and disclaimers
- no explicit photos in public-facing materials
- engineering-focused product language

Estimated first buy: $300 to $1,500

Do not assume Stripe, PayPal, Etsy, Amazon, or Shopify Payments will accept the category without restriction. Verify before building the sales channel around one processor.

## Labor Plan for 100 Units/Week

### Owner-Only Production

Possible but tight.

Daily work:

- morning demold/trim/inspect
- prep molds
- first pour
- packaging from previous batch
- second pour
- clean tools
- inventory count
- customer messages

Owner-only can work for the first few weeks if orders are predictable and the product is simple.

### Owner + One Helper

Recommended once demand is real.

Helper tasks:

- trim flash
- wash/dry molds
- prep packaging
- label bags
- assemble kits
- inventory count

At 100 units/week, one helper can reduce chaos more than one extra machine.

## First 100 Units/Week Setup

Minimum setup:

- 12 to 15 one-cavity molds
- enough material for 150 units
- packaging for 150 units
- 20% scrap budget
- one drying/curing shelf
- one inspection table
- one packaging table
- clear batch numbering

Daily rhythm:

1. Demold yesterday/previous cycle.
2. Inspect each part.
3. Trim flash.
4. Bag accepted units.
5. Record rejects and failure reason.
6. Clean/prep molds.
7. Pour next batch.
8. Package and ship accepted units.

## Quality Control

Each unit gets:

- batch number
- mold number
- visual inspection
- pull/retention check if applicable
- surface tack check
- cure check
- packaging check

Reject if:

- uncured/tacky
- bubbles in critical areas
- torn flange
- weak collar edge
- contaminated surface
- bad trim
- wrong hardness
- visible defect in contact area

## Customer Discovery Before Scaling

Before buying material for 1,000 units, validate:

- Would owners buy a passive replacement insert?
- Would fixed-opening owners pay for retrofit assessment?
- Would they ship a doll/torso for controlled installation?
- What price is acceptable?
- What failure would make them demand a refund?
- What brands/models are most common?

Goal: 10 to 25 owner interviews before the first 100-unit batch.

## Business Risk Ranking

Top risks:

1. Material compatibility failure
2. Tear failure around modified opening
3. Payment processor restrictions
4. Customer installation mistakes
5. Hygiene/liability claims
6. Low repeat purchase rate
7. Demand exists but not at profitable price
8. Shipping/privacy concerns
9. Public platform restrictions
10. Copycat sellers after proof of demand

## Minimum Practical Launch Decision

If cash is under $3,000:

- do not aim for 100/week yet
- make test blocks and 10 prototypes only
- validate demand first

If cash is $5,000 to $8,000:

- build garage production for passive inserts or cleaning kits
- target 100/week only after molds are stable

If cash is $12,000 to $18,000:

- build the first real 100/week passive production cell
- run customer validation and small-batch sales

If cash is $20,000+:

- passive product plus retrofit collar testing
- powered 5V prototype development can begin in parallel, but not as the first launch product

## Final Recommendation

Minimum path to 100 units/week:

1. Start with passive insert or cleaning/drying kit.
2. Make 12 to 15 simple one-cavity molds.
3. Produce 20 good units/day for 5 days.
4. Track rejects by mold number.
5. Sell quietly through a landing page/waitlist/customer discovery channel.
6. Use early buyers to validate the retrofit collar/socket system.
7. Only then move into powered 5V inserts and full retrofit kits.

The cheapest real launch is not the fanciest product.

The cheapest real launch is the simplest product that proves buyers exist and teaches the material system.

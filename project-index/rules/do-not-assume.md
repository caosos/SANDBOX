# Do Not Assume Rules

These rules exist so future sessions do not restart from generic advice.

## Global rules

- Preserve Michael's exact key words when the wording matters.
- Do not replace technical, moral, political, legal, religious, or emotionally meaningful words with softened/intensified synonyms.
- Do not turn critique of an idea into critique of people.
- Do not infer from generic internet knowledge when project-specific GitHub/sandbox/conversation material exists.
- Verify before claiming something is verified.
- When uncertain, say so and inspect the source material.

## Dating service rules

- Do not call it a normal dating app unless Michael does.
- Do not make swipe mechanics the default.
- Do not lead with photos.
- Do not reduce the system to a personality quiz.
- Do not assume high match volume is the goal.
- Start with criteria, compatibility, trust, and profile truthfulness.

## CAOSCare rules

- Do not disturb the Lenovo/Sony/Roku setup for HP/CAOSCare node work.
- eMeet belongs to the CAOSCare node, not the final Lenovo workstation audio path.
- Treat building systems like fire panels as vendor-supported/read-only integration targets, not hack targets.
- Senior-care context requires safety and reliability.

## Hardware rules

- Main workstation: Lenovo laptop -> HDMI -> Sony KDL-55EX640 -> Roku Streambar audio target.
- CAOSCare node: HP Elite -> separate monitor/Ethernet/SSH -> eMeet.
- Do not mix those hardware paths unless Michael explicitly asks.

## Business engine rules

- Start small: scan footprint, fix what is missing, keep it updated.
- Keep customer-facing reports simple: good/bad/fixable/customer action.
- Local resource routing matters.
